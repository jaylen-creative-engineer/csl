# Product vision

Direction for CSL as a self-learning and collective competitive platform: proof-of-skill, cohort energy, and phased engineering ([[lat.md/work-graph#Work graph#Phases (execution spine)]]).

## North star

Creative Sports League is a **structured, public proof-of-skill** layer for emerging creatives: challenge sprints produce artifacts, scoring turns effort into **signals**, and leagues give hosts a repeatable **content and cohort engine**.

The long-term shape is a **self-learning platform** — individual value (portfolio, feedback, progression) on one path, and a **collective** space that is **collaborative** (cohorts, shared briefs, peer visibility) and **competitive** (rankings, leaderboards, sponsor stakes).

## Individual learner journey

How single learners gain value from the product today and what is still mostly roadmap.

Today the domain model already supports:

- Public **portfolio** from submissions ([[lat.md/domain-model#Domain model#Entities#Showcase Intelligence]])
- **Skill signals** derived from criteria-weighted scores, not self-claims
- **Leaderboard** as a reputation anchor within a league

**Gap toward “self-learning”:** persistence, accounts, personalized paths, and longitudinal progression are staged under [[lat.md/work-graph#Work graph#Work themes#Theme — Individual progression & learning loops]].

## Collective playground

Collaboration is implicit today (shared leagues and showcase); competition is explicit (leaderboard, top performers).

**Gap:** teams, peer review, cohort critique, and richer “playground” formats are captured under [[lat.md/work-graph#Work graph#Work themes#Theme — Social & collective layers]].

## Platform evolution

Evolution follows [[lat.md/work-graph#Work graph#Phases (execution spine)]]: persistence and APIs, then UX, then auth — then personalization and social layers compound.
