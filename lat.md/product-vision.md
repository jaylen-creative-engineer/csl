# Product vision

Direction for CSL as a hero tool for creatives and technologists: long-range creative vision, strategic execution, proof-of-skill, cohort energy, and phased rollout ([[lat.md/work-graph#Work graph#Phases (execution spine)]], [[lat.md/rollout-strategy#Rollout strategy]]).

## North star

Creative Sports League is the **hero tool** for creatives, technologists, analysts, and synthesizers — enabling them to define a long-range creative vision, establish strategy, and execute through real-life and digital interaction patterns.

The tool balances **future vision** and **daily execution** in ways that make the most of wellness, relationships, and the execution factor. Challenge sprints produce artifacts, scoring turns effort into **signals**, and leagues give hosts a repeatable **content and cohort engine**.

The long-term shape is a **self-learning platform** — individual value (portfolio, feedback, progression) on one path, and a **collective** space that is **collaborative** (cohorts, shared briefs, peer visibility) and **competitive** (rankings, leaderboards, sponsor stakes).

## Hero tool thesis

CSL serves the capable creative or technologist who can deliver but lacks access to traditional networks and warm relationships.

Gatekeeping and obscurity lead to hidden genius — talented individuals whose capabilities are not fully leveraged by society, and whose work cannot be found by the investment and organizations that would benefit from it. This tool targets that use case directly. It gives individuals a structured, public, meritocratic path to **demonstrate capability**, **get feedback**, and **find the next big moments to execute** — without requiring pre-existing connections or insider access.

## Two interaction patterns

CSL ships as two complementary modes that serve different stages of adoption ([[lat.md/rollout-strategy#Rollout strategy#Interaction patterns]]):

### Single-player mode

The open-source, self-hosted experience: download the tool, add an API key, and the environment is ready ([[lat.md/rollout-strategy#Rollout strategy#Interaction patterns#Single-player mode (open source)]]).

An individual pursuing long-range goals, executing strategically throughout the year, using the platform to hold the balance between vision and execution. Maps directly to [[lat.md/individual-learner-journey#Individual learner journey#Behavior vision]].

### Network mode

Communities join and interact within the Creative Sports League network, surfacing talent through demonstrated work.

People demonstrate capability, receive feedback, and discover moments to execute. Leagues, hosts, sponsors, and the collective playground come alive. Network mode removes the gatekeeping layer: talent becomes visible through work, not through who you know.

## Individual learner journey

Solo learners pursue **skill X** via mastery-oriented guidance: AI-coordinated frameworks, plans, paths, resources, accountability, plus optional social collaboration and competition ([[lat.md/individual-learner-journey#Individual learner journey#Behavior vision]]).

Details on feature areas live in [[lat.md/individual-learner-journey]]. **In product today:** portfolio and skill signals from scored work; leaderboard as competitive anchor ([[lat.md/current-system#Current system#Capabilities implemented]]).

**Gap:** persistence, learner identity, AI orchestration, structured plans, and accountability UX ship incrementally — see [[lat.md/work-graph#Work graph#Work themes#Theme — Individual progression & learning loops]].

## Collective playground

Collaboration is implicit today (shared leagues and showcase); competition is explicit (leaderboard, top performers).

**Gap:** teams, peer review, cohort critique, and richer "playground" formats are captured under [[lat.md/work-graph#Work graph#Work themes#Theme — Social & collective layers]].

## First-person hero use case

The founder is the first hero user — leveraging CSL as a creative technologist for long-range goals and daily execution.

The Q3/Q4 public announcement ([[lat.md/rollout-strategy#Rollout strategy#Announcement timeline]]) positions this lived experience as the proof case before wider adoption. This first-person narrative is the anchor for the product story: one person used the tool, it worked, here is the evidence — now it is available to everyone.
